

What is Grand Theft Auto?

Grand Theft Auto (GTA) is a series of action-adventure video games developed by Rockstar Games. The games are set in fictional locales that parody cities, such as Vice City, San Andreas and Liberty City, all of which are based on real world locations. Players take the role of criminals who are trying to rise through the ranks of the criminal underworld. Players are able to roam through the open world and can partake in a variety of activities, such as car-jacking, vigilante missions, racing and more.

Grand Theft Auto is the most successful video game franchise of all time, with over 150 million game units sold and billions of dollars in revenue. The games have been praised for their open world designs, detailed graphics, and their tongue-in-cheek humor. They have also been criticized by some for their violence, moral ambiguity, and themes of misogyny. The series has spawned a massive amount of media, including movies, soundtracks, comic books, and even a television series.

A Brief History of Grand Theft Auto

The original Grand Theft Auto video game was released in Europe on October 22nd, 1997. Developed by DMA Design for the PlayStation, the game quickly grew in popularity, sparked by a controversial advertising campaign. The game followed a mobster as he tried to climb the ladder in the criminal underworld of a fictional city by completing missions for different criminal organizations.

The success of the original game led to the creation of a series of sequels, each set in a different location. Grand Theft Auto 2, released in 1999, was set in a futuristic city and featured a larger world map and improved gameplay. Grand Theft Auto III, released in 2001, was the first of the series to be released on the Xbox as well as the PlayStation. Grand Theft Auto III added to the series' open world gameplay by introducing fully 3D graphics and a wide range of vehicles.

Following the success of Grand Theft Auto III, Rockstar Games, who had since taken over the franchise, released several more titles in the series. These included Vice City, San Andreas and Liberty City. Each game was set in a different city, with new vehicles, weapons, and missions. These games were enhanced over time with additional content, such as downloadable packs and expansions.

Grand Theft Auto IV, released in 2008, was the first game in the series to be released on the Xbox 360 and the PlayStation 3. The game followed the story of Niko Bellic, an immigrant from Eastern Europe, as he tries to survive in the fictional Liberty City. The game added several new features to the series, such as an online multiplayer mode.

Grand Theft Auto: Chinatown Wars was released in 2009 and was released for both the Nintendo DS and the PSP. The game was praised for its use of the systems' stylus and touch-screen capabilities.

Grand Theft Auto V was released in 2013 for the Xbox 360 and PlayStation 3, and was later released for the Xbox One and PlayStation 4 in 2014. The game is set in the fictional city of Los Santos, and follows three protagonists as they try to work together to survive in the criminal underworld. Grand Theft Auto V also introduced the series to the first-person perspective and improved the series' online multiplayer mode.

Conclusion

Grand Theft Auto is a popular video game franchise that has been around since 1997. The series has seen several iterations over the years, each with its own unique take on the open world action-adventure genre. The series has been applauded for its visuals, humor, and open world gameplay. It has also been criticized for its violence, moral ambiguity, and themes of misogyny. Throughout its 20 years of existence, it has become one of the most recognizable and beloved video game franchises of all time.
